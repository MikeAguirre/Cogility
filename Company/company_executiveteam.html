Executive Team

Led by professionals with both technical and business consulting experience and a technical leadership team with years of experience developing system and business system engineering methods. With thought leadership in system architecture we design complex, end-to-end and solve your application integration problems.

Jim Drumright,

Chief Executive Officer

Jim has been a sales executive at multiple hardware and software companies over the last 40 years, developing enterprise sales organizations, both in the US and the Far East. Jim began his career at IBM and went through its Systems Engineering and Sales Training School. He spent almost 15 years at Burroughs Corporation as a branch manager, product manager, and General Manager in the Far East. Jim then moved to Tolerant Systems as VP International Sales and then to Pyramid Technology as Director of Sales. After many successful years selling hardware, Jim moved into software sales with BEA and was soon promoted to VP Sales Western Region. Within five years, he had grown the Western Region Sales Organization to 50 Sales Executives and seven District Managers. Sales revenues increased from $3 Million the first year to $225 Million in the fifth year, with the highest productivity per sales person in the company.

Dale Shipley,

Senior Vice President, Corporate Strategy

Dale has a career that spans over forty years in the high technology industry. Starting at General Electric’s mainframe computer business as a computer operator, he went on to become a world expert on system software in operating systems, online transaction processing and networking. Since that time he has founded or co-founded three companies. He was a co-founder of Tolerant Systems, where he served as Vice President of Engineering and Chief Technology Officer. Later, as a founder of VERITAS Software, he secured the contract with AT&T Bell Labs that lead to VERITAS Storage Management Products becoming an industry standard for UNIX based computer systems. VERITAS was merged into Symantec Software in 2005. At DSG Technologies, he was co-founder and Chairman of the Board. He is also serving as the Chief Strategist for ConductorLive.

Martin Artiano,

Senior Vice President, Business Development

Martin has more than 25 years successful experience representing, founding, building and selling advanced technology firms. From 1981-1990 he was Senior Partner of the Washington, DC based law firm Anderson, Hibey, Nauheim and Blair, where he specialized in representation of Fortune 100 companies in connection with Federal contracting and administrative issues. Martin also has extensive experience in transactional law. From 1990-2002, he was a co-founder and served as EVP of two advanced technology firms, the latter based in Silicon Valley, both of which were profitably sold. Martin also served as a Deputy Director of President Reagan's 1980 campaign.

Michael Latta,

CTO

Michael is responsible for leading the technical architecture of products and engagements, and intellectual property development. He is a thought leader in Model Driven Systems, Systems Engineering and Software Architectures. He has over thirty five years of experience in model driven software, software and systems development, project leadership, and cutting edge technology innovation. In his 12 years with Cogility he has lead the development of the product effort from inception to the current offering, including the design of our patented Complex Event Processing features. He has been involved with every aspect of the product and most of our customer engagements. As a leader in the company’s technology, he is routinely involved in the most demanding and challenging customer engagements ensuring value delivered, and that product guidance is coupled to customer needs. Michael has over a decade of federal project experience in the intelligence community, including Cogility JIEDDO, Air Force, and Army engagements, as well as prior Intel projects. Michael has a B.S. in Computer Science from the California State University at Sacramento, School of Engineering.
